About

Plant–microbe interactions at the molecular scale.

I am a master's candidate in plant molecular biology, currently completing my thesis internship on plant–PGPM (Plant Growth-Promoting Microorganism) interactions.

My work sits at the intersection of plant immunity, rhizosphere ecology, and molecular signalling. I am interested in understanding how beneficial microorganisms modulate plant developmental and defence programmes — from the molecular dialogue at the root interface to the downstream transcriptional responses that shape plant fitness.

Research

Interests & Focus Areas

Plant–PGPM Molecular Dialogue

Signalling mechanisms underlying beneficial plant–microorganism interactions, including MAMP perception and systemic resistance induction.

Rhizosphere Microbiology

Root exudate composition, microbiome recruitment, and the functional ecology of growth-promoting bacteria and fungi.

Plant Molecular Biology

Transcriptional regulation of defence and growth pathways; hormone crosstalk (SA, JA, auxin) in the context of plant–microbe symbiosis.

Sustainable Agriculture

Translational applications of PGPM research toward reduced agrochemical dependence and improved crop resilience under abiotic stress.
